Black Coffee

The popular thing it seems now is to dress coffee up as much as possible. I still drink my coffee black, I start with it whole bean, and I do not use an electric coffee maker. I drink a lot of coffee, so you would think I would dress it up sometime, but here I am, enjoying my boring and flavorless cup of mud.

Now you'll need a coffee grinder, and a french press. I use an electric kettle to warm my water, but you can always do it on a stove. I also only ever make one cup at a time. When purchasing a french press, it normally includes a scoop and estimates how much coffee you will need. I typically use a bit more than the recommended dose. It's just trial and error - do what tastes best for you!

Here are the steps I take:

  1. Grind the beans with a grinder
  2. Get your water warming up, either in a kettle or on the stove
  3. Put your scoop(s) of coffee grounds in the bottom of the french press
  4. Give the water a second to cool once it is brought to boil
  5. Most french presses recommend letting it brew in the french press for about four minutes, but you can leave it longer if you want it stronger
  6. Enjoy your coffee! Cream and suger are added calories, so I avoid all of that
